🍼 The under-3 reality check

Your group is unusual: all four kids are 5 or under, and two are just 1 and 2. Most "family resort" praise targets the 4–12 sweet spot — big slides, lazy rivers, kids' clubs from age 4 — which does little for a 1-year-old. So this tool scores the things that actually matter at ages 1–3: can you drop a baby off, are there shallow splash zones (not just tall slides), are rooms nap-friendly, and what unsponsored parents say online. Scored honestly, the resorts with real baby care (Club Med, Palladium) rise; the ones that are gorgeous-but-hands-on (Finest, Ziva) sit lower — even if their photos win Instagram.

The dossiers

One card per resort, with the honest verdict for your group.

Three things to confirm before booking these exact dates. (1) Under-3 club hours, capacity & pricing — these change and fill up, especially at Club Med where Baby/Petit Club is paid and limited. (2) Early-August sargassum seaweed — Cap Cana (Ziva) and Bávaro reports are mixed; all the open-ocean beaches here can get weed in summer, raked off each morning. (3) That "toddler water" scores reward shallow splash zones for a 1–3 year-old, not big slides/lazy rivers (which skew older). On social/TikTok: treat agent-posted "top 3 resort!" clips with caution — much of Ziva's buzz is travel-agent marketing; the most useful signal is unsponsored parent reviews, which is what these notes lean on.
